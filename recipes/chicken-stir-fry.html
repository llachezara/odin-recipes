Chicken Stir-Fry

Description

This quick and easy chicken stir-fry is packed with colorful vegetables and coated in a savory sauce. It's a healthy, delicious meal that comes together in under 30 minutes.

Perfect for busy weeknights, this versatile dish can be customized with whatever vegetables you have on hand. Serve it over rice or noodles for a complete meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into cubes
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, cornstarch, and chicken broth. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Add chicken pieces and season with salt and pepper. Cook until golden brown, about 5-6 minutes.
  4. Remove chicken and set aside.
  5. In the same pan, add garlic and ginger. Stir-fry for 30 seconds.
  6. Add broccoli, bell pepper, and carrots. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes.
  7. Add snap peas and cook for another 2 minutes.
  8. Return chicken to the pan.
  9. Pour in the sauce mixture and cook until it thickens, about 2-3 minutes.
  10. Serve hot over rice or noodles.
